# Academic Writing Guide for Engineering Reports

## Structure
A standard PFE report includes:
1. **Abstract**: 150-250 words summarizing the entire project
2. **Introduction**: Problem statement, objectives, and scope
3. **Literature Review**: Analysis of existing work
4. **Methodology**: Approach and methods used
5. **Results and Discussion**: Findings with analysis
6. **Conclusion**: Summary and future work
7. **References**: Properly formatted citations

## Writing Style
- Use formal, objective language
- Avoid personal pronouns (use "this study" not "I")
- Be precise and specific
- Support claims with evidence

## Technical Elements
- Use figures and tables to present data clearly
- Label all visual elements with numbers and captions
- Cross-reference figures in the text
- Include units in all measurements

## Language Guidelines
- Use past tense for completed work
- Use present tense for general truths
- Avoid contractions (use "do not" not "don't")
- Spell out acronyms on first use

## Common Issues
- **Vague language**: Replace "significant" with specific metrics
- **Passive overuse**: Mix active and passive voice appropriately
- **Citation errors**: Use consistent citation format throughout
- **Plagiarism**: Always cite sources and paraphrase properly

## Proofreading Checklist
- [ ] Check spelling and grammar
- [ ] Verify all citations are complete
- [ ] Ensure figure labels are correct
- [ ] Check numbering of sections
- [ ] Verify units and formatting consistency
