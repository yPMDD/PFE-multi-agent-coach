# Guide to PFE Methodology

## Overview
The Projet de Fin d'Etudes (PFE) is the culminating project for engineering students. It demonstrates your ability to apply learned concepts to a real-world engineering challenge.

## Selecting a Topic
1. Choose a topic aligned with your specialization
2. Ensure it has clear objectives and scope
3. Consider available resources and timeline

## Research Phase
- Conduct a literature review of at least 20 relevant sources
- Identify gaps in existing research
- Define your research questions clearly
- Document all sources properly using academic citation style

## Methodology Types
- **Experimental**: Testing hypotheses through controlled experiments
- **Analytical**: Using mathematical modeling and simulations
- **Design**: Creating solutions to defined problems
- **Case Study**: In-depth analysis of specific instances

## Implementation
- Break down your project into manageable phases
- Set milestones with realistic deadlines
- Keep a detailed project log
- Document all decisions and changes

## Validation
- Test your solution against defined criteria
- Compare results with theoretical expectations
- Analyze discrepancies and provide explanations
- Seek feedback from your supervisor regularly

## Common Mistakes to Avoid
- Starting without a clear problem statement
- Underestimating time required for each phase
- Neglecting regular progress documentation
- Waiting until the last minute to start writing
- Not seeking feedback early and often
